\section*{Abstract}
\todo[inline]{Reescribir}
The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence and the increasing complexity of deep neural networks (DNNs) have significantly increased the demand for specialized hardware accelerators capable of efficiently executing these workloads. However, the design of such accelerators remains a complex task, involving numerous architectural decisions, such as the exploration of a vast design space under power, performance, and area (PPA) constraints. These challenges typically require significant expertise and long development cycles.

This thesis presents \textbf{TALOS}(Target-driven Architecture Layer
Optimization System), a framework aimed at simplifying and accelerating the design of DNN hardware accelerators. TALOS follows a two-stage methodology. First, a design space exploration (DSE) stage evaluates different architectural configurations for a given DNN workload in order to identify promising accelerator designs. Then, the selected configuration is provided to a hardware generation stage capable of constructing the corresponding accelerator implementation using a set of hardware building blocks.

By bridging architecture exploration and hardware generation, TALOS aims to streamline the accelerator design process and reduce the time required to obtain efficient domain-specific hardware implementations for DNN workloads.
